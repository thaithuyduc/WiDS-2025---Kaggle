# WiDS Datathon 2025

## Overview

The Women in Data Science (WiDS) Datathon is a global machine learning competition organized by the WiDS Worldwide initiative. The competition aims to encourage participation in data science while addressing real-world challenges through predictive modeling and data-driven solutions.

Each year, participants are provided with a curated dataset and are tasked with building machine learning models to solve a socially impactful problem. The competition emphasizes collaboration, innovation, and the practical application of data science techniques.

The competition is on Kaggle: https://www.kaggle.com/competitions/widsdatathon2025

## Competition Objective

The objective of the WiDS Datathon 2025 is to develop machine learning models that improve our understanding of Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) in children and adolescents. Participants are challenged to build predictive models using behavioral, demographic, and neuroimaging-related data.

The competition consists of two prediction tasks:

1. ADHD Prediction – Determine whether an individual has ADHD based on the provided features.
2. Sex Prediction – Predict whether the individual is male or female.

By addressing these tasks, the competition aims to advance research on ADHD and explore potential sex-related differences in its presentation, ultimately contributing to more equitable and effective healthcare outcomes.

## Dataset

The dataset contains a collection of structured features describing the characteristics of the studied population. These features may include demographic, behavioral, environmental, or domain-specific attributes depending on the year’s challenge.

The dataset is divided into:

* Training Set: Used for model development and validation.
* Test Set: Used for generating predictions submitted to Kaggle.
* Data Dictionary: Provides descriptions and definitions for each feature.
